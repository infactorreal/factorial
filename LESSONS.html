PERMUTATION: The arrangement of all part of set of objects with regard to the order of the arrangement.

P (n,r)= n!/(n-r)!

EXAMPLE:

Kim, Maja, Angel, Kat and Erich is in a race. How many possible ways of 1st, 2nd, 3rd places can come out of this race?

5P3

= 5!/((5-3)!)

= 120/2

= 60 ways




COMBINATION: Selection of items with no order.

P (n,r)= n!/(r! x (n-r)!)

EXAMPLE:

Kim, Maja, Angel, Kat and Erich are considered to join I.T Olympics. Only 3 students are going to be choosen.

How many possible combinations can be made?

5C3

= 5!/((3!) x (2!))

= 120/2

= 60 ways




PERMUTATION WITH REPETITION: Applicable for partitioning or groupings of all the n objects.

(n/(n_1,n_2…n_k ))= n!/(n_1 !×n_2…×n_k !)







SPECIAL CASES FOR PERMUTATION

  1. A. Clustering/Grouping – objects must stay together

    1. Dan has 4 diff. Physics textbooks and 3 diff. Math books on his shelf. How many arrangements of these books can be made if the Math books must be kept together?

      (5!) x (3!) = 120 x 6 = 720 ways

      5 is derived from P1 P2 P3 P4 M3, as M3 is TREATED AS ONE OBJECT


  2. B. Complement of Clustering/ Grouping – doesn’t care about the arrangement

    1. How many ways can the letters of the word NOJACK be arranged if the letters ACK must be kept together in that order?

      4! = 24 ways

      4 is derived from L1 L2 L3 ACK, as ACK is TREATED AS ONE OBJECT
